Waiting is a part of the natural rhythm of life… but depending on what you’re waiting for, waiting seasons can seem to last a lifetime, can’t they?
In high school, we wait ‘til we’re old enough for a job or to start dating. In college, we may wait anxiously for graduation. Sometimes, we wait on getting married—or finding a new job—or having a baby—or healing from an illness. Often, we wait on God’s direction for small and big decisions.
The people of God throughout the ages are no strangers to waiting. Ever since sin entered the world in the Garden of Eden, humanity has been waiting. First, for a Savior to come—now, for our Savior to return.
This Christmas season, let’s pause and remember the first coming of Christ, the long-awaited Messiah who would deliver His people from their sins. Scripture says that in God’s perfect timing, Jesus was born at just the right moment to save us:
“But when the fullness of time had come, God sent forth his Son, born of woman, born under the law, to redeem those who were under the law, so that we might receive adoption as sons.”
Galatians 4:4–5
Over the next several days, we’ll spend time remembering God’s promises and encourage our hearts: We can trust His timing!
Starting tomorrow, you’ll receive an email to your inbox with the challenge for the day. Take a few moments each day to dwell on the truth of Scripture and rest in God’s plan for your life.
As we prepare to celebrate the birth of Christ, we hope this challenge is an opportunity for you to rejoice in our Savior—and look forward to when He’ll come back and make all things new. In the meantime, each day is an invitation to remember God’s promises and trust Him as we wait.
— The Yarrow team
