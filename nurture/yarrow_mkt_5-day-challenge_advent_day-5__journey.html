Over the past few days, we’ve seen how in every generation God reassured His people of His promises and worked to bring about the fulfillment of His good, good plans.
But we also have to acknowledge the tension we currently live with. Although we have the privilege of living after Jesus has come, we’re still waiting—waiting for Him to come back. Waiting for the complete fulfillment of God’s promises to restore the world, eradicate sin, conquer death, and make all things new through King Jesus.
Read 1 Timothy 6:13–15, where Paul gives his protégé, Timothy, some final instructions for living faithfully as we wait for Jesus to come back. Pay close attention to each reference to Jesus.
I charge you in the presence of God, who gives life to all things, and of Christ Jesus, who in his testimony before Pontius Pilate made the good confession, to keep the commandment unstained and free from reproach until the appearing of our Lord Jesus Christ, which he will display at the proper time—he who is the blessed and only Sovereign, the King of kings and Lord of lords…
Now, ask yourself these questions:
  1. When does Paul say Christ’s “appearing” (a reference to His second coming) will be?
  2. What does Paul call Timothy—and us—to do as we wait for Christ to come back?
  3. As this challenge wraps up, what are you still waiting on God for?
We serve a God who kept His promise to send a Savior, a King from the tribe of Judah and from the line of David, to deliver His people from their sins. God has also promised that King Jesus will one day reign forever on Earth, having restored all of creation.
As we celebrate the birth of Christ this Christmas season, we can be encouraged that the same God who fulfilled His promise to send a Savior will also provide for us today as we wait on Him. We can trust His timing!
To wrap-up today, here’s a guided prayer. Feel free to tweak this or use your own words!
Pray
God, thank You that You sent Jesus to the world at just the right moment. And one day soon He will come back “at the proper time.” As I wait on You in my own life, remind me I can trust Your timing. You are the blessed and only Sovereign, the King of kings and Lord of lords. You will take care of me. In Jesus’ name, amen.
